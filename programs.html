Early Learning Rooted in Care, Faith, and Growth

At Poplar Christian Learning Academy, each program is designed to support children at their current stage of development. Through warm relationships, meaningful play, guided exploration, and a nurturing Christian environment, children are encouraged to grow socially, emotionally, spiritually, physically, and academically.

Sunflowers — Infants

Infants

The Sunflowers infant program provides a calm, responsive, and nurturing environment where babies are cared for with patience, gentleness, and close attention. Infants are supported through safe exploration, sensory discovery, consistent routines, and warm interaction with trusted caregivers.

What children experience

  • Gentle, responsive care
  • Safe spaces for movement and exploration
  • Sensory experiences with colors, textures, sounds, and familiar objects
  • Meaningful interaction with caregivers at the child’s level
  • Consistent routines that support comfort and security

Development focus

  • Trust and attachment
  • Early communication
  • Sensory awareness
  • Motor development
  • Social and emotional security

Little Sprouts — 1 Year Olds

1 Year Olds

The Little Sprouts program supports one-year-olds as they become more curious, mobile, expressive, and eager to explore. Children are encouraged to discover the world around them through guided play, music, movement, simple language activities, and hands-on experiences.

What children experience

  • Play-based exploration
  • Songs, stories, and early language activities
  • Opportunities for movement and coordination
  • Soft, safe, and engaging classroom spaces
  • Outdoor play as weather allows
  • Warm guidance from attentive teachers

Development focus

  • Language growth
  • Social connection
  • Early independence
  • Fine and gross motor skills
  • Curiosity and confidence

Rosebuds — 2 & 3 Year Olds

2 & 3 Year Olds

The Rosebuds program supports two- and three-year-olds as they build independence, communication skills, confidence, and social awareness. Teachers provide structure, encouragement, and gentle guidance while allowing children to make choices, practice new skills, and grow through age-appropriate activities.

What children experience

  • Creative play and hands-on learning
  • Simple routines that build responsibility
  • Guided language and communication support
  • Opportunities to make choices
  • Social interaction with peers
  • Encouragement as they practice independence

Development focus

  • Communication and vocabulary
  • Self-help skills
  • Emotional expression
  • Cooperation and sharing
  • Confidence and classroom readiness

Preschool Readiness

Older Toddlers & Preschool-Aged Children

PCLA encourages preschool readiness by helping children strengthen the skills they need for the next stage of learning. Children are supported through activities that build confidence, creativity, listening skills, early problem-solving, social development, and independence.

What children experience

  • Early literacy and language-rich activities
  • Counting, sorting, matching, and early math concepts
  • Creative expression through art, music, and movement
  • Classroom routines that encourage responsibility
  • Social learning through group activities
  • Encouragement to listen, follow directions, and participate

Development focus

  • School readiness
  • Early literacy and numeracy
  • Attention and listening
  • Creativity
  • Confidence and independence
  • Positive peer interaction

A Nurturing Daily Environment

Each classroom is designed to help children feel comfortable, safe, and ready to participate. Children are supported through predictable routines, teacher interaction, play, rest, meals or snacks, outdoor time as weather allows, and activities that match their developmental stage.

Safe & Supportive Classrooms

Children are cared for in spaces designed to encourage comfort, safety, movement, and age-appropriate exploration.

Faith-Centered Care

PCLA’s environment reflects Christian values through love, patience, kindness, respect, and encouragement.

Meaningful Play

Play is used as a foundation for learning, helping children build language, creativity, motor skills, and social confidence.
